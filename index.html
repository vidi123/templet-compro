STEEL PRODUCTION AND SCRAP CONSUMPTION

In 2019, roughly 1.5 billion metric tons of steel were produced in only seven regions, with Chinaproducing the majorityat 996 million tons of steel. Globally, the consumption of steel scrap increased by 3.4 percent between 2018 and 2019.

Since the first industrial revolution, steel has been one of the key materials used in industrial production. With environmental awareness on the rise, companies are now prioritizing the use of recycled metals in the production process.

Used metals are crushed and shredded into scraps which can be conveniently transported and used in the production cycle. China is the leading user of steel scrap worldwide. Among the leading exporters of steel scrap, the U.S. exported over 24 million metric tons in 2011, whichdecreasedto 18 million metrictonsin2019.
